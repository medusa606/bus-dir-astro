sections:
  - heading: The sourcing question
    body: The best steakhouses in Bristol are transparent about their supply chain — often naming the farm, the breed, and the aging period. Native breeds like Dexter, Longhorn, and Aberdeen Angus have more intramuscular fat and a more complex flavour than commercially reared alternatives, and the difference is noticeable on the plate. If a restaurant can't tell you where their beef comes from, that's a reasonable signal about priorities.
  - heading: Getting the cook right
    body: A great piece of beef deserves to be cooked to the right temperature and rested properly before it reaches the plate. The places on this list understand that, and they'll push back if you order a well-done piece of dry-aged ribeye — gently, but still. That's usually a good sign. The right answer for most cuts is medium-rare; the best spots will guide you if you're not sure.
