sections:
  - heading: The Neapolitan question
    body: Neapolitan-style pizza — thin base, leopard-spotted crust, briefly and intensely baked — has become the benchmark for serious pizza in the UK, and Bristol has a few places doing it properly. The key markers are a charred but not bitter crust, a base that doesn't sag under the topping weight, and ingredients that haven't been over-processed. When it works, it's one of the most satisfying meals you can have.
  - heading: Beyond the classics
    body: Not every great pizza in Bristol is Neapolitan. Some of the best places are doing their own thing — sourdough bases with longer ferments, unconventional toppings that actually work, and a house style that's clearly been thought about rather than copied. These spots tend to divide opinion more, but the ones that have earned consistent praise from locals are worth visiting on their own terms.
